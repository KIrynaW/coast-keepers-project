Our Story

We are a group of friends that have been living and surfing together on Ballybunion beach for over ten years.In 2022, after a long break from socialising, we met up for our first surf after the covid pandemic.

We have noticed a lot of plastic debris washing up at the beach; especially surgical masks and plastic capsules. We have always been passionate about our environments and the beaches, thus we came up with the Coast Keepers initiative.

We also want to encourage people to start engaging with local community again while cleaning up our beautiful North Kerry beaches and learning about the harm of coastal pollution.

Why is coast cleaning so important?

There are many causes of the degradation of marine ecology: humans pollute beaches with various single use plastics, fishing gear, surgical masks etc. Also, marine debries consisting of plastic and chemical polutants are carried from all over the world and accumulate on beaches. This often leads to environmental degradation


These pollutants harm marine life, ecology, human health, and coastal tourism

Public Health

Polluted beaches can harm human lives; many items such as broken glasses, sharp metals, or hard plastics may injure beach-goers physically. Plastic and chemical polutants can contaminate the water, exposure to which can lead to health issues.

Marine Life Protection

Beach litter poses dangers to wildlife on the beaches and marine ecology. Many beach pollutants such as fishing gears and nets harm sea animals including sea turtles and seabirds, and can cause serious injuries or death if the animal gets trapped in them.

Preventing Polution

Clean beaches are indicators of the environmental quality.Trash on the beach, if left untouched, can make it's way into the waterways;by cleaning it from our coasts we prevent harmful pollutants from entering the marine environment.

Coast Conservation

.Ecosystems on our coasts are very intricate and disruption to their delicate systems is detrimental to their welbeeing.Cleaning beaches from trash is vital to keeping the balance of marine biodiversity and ensuring the survival and helth of local ecosystems and species.
